# Smaran — Interview Guide

> **Companion to**: `RESEARCH_PLAN.md` · **Length**: 45-60 min · **Format**: Semi-structured, conducted by Rev, recorded with consent.

---

## How to use this guide

This is a **guide, not a script.** Read every question as a doorway, not a checkbox. Follow the energy of the conversation. The probes in italics are there if the participant gives a one-line answer — use them to dig.

**Rev's job during the interview:**
- Talk 30%, listen 70%.
- Never validate ("yes, exactly!" → biases them). Use "tell me more" and silence.
- Never pitch Smaran in sections 1-3. The concept reveal is in section 4 for a reason.
- Capture verbatim quotes — your synthesis lives or dies on real language.

**Recording**: explicit consent, on camera or audio. "I'm recording so I can come back to your words later. Only the team will hear it. Is that OK?"

## Section 1 — Warm-up (5 min)

> Goal: rapport, set tone, lower stakes.

1. Tell me about your day so far. *(low-stakes opener)*
2. Where are you living, and how long have you been there?
3. What do you do for work / school?

> Casual. Smile. Then transition:
> *"I'm working on something in the devotional / spiritual space, and before I tell you about it, I'd love to just understand how you live. There are no wrong answers — I'm trying to learn."*

---

## Section 2 — Context: current devotional life (10 min)

> Goal: understand their actual practice today, in their words, without leading.

4. Walk me through your morning. Anything devotional, reflective, or quiet in it?
   - *Probe: When did you last do that? Yesterday? Last week?*
   - *Probe: How long has that part of your morning looked like that?*

5. When did your current spiritual or devotional practice take its current shape — was it always like this, or did something change?
   - *Probe: A teacher, a death, a job change, a kid being born — any inflection point?*

6. How much does devotion show up in your week vs every day? Be honest.
   - *Probe: What about festivals — does the volume change?*

7. Sanskrit, the Gita, scripture — how much exposure do you have, and where did it come from?
   - *Probe: At home? School? A specific guru? YouTube?*

8. If you had to put a label on your relationship with Hinduism / Sanatan dharma — what feels right?
   - *Probe: practising / cultural / curious / lapsed / seeker / something else?*

---

## Section 3 — Deep dive: needs, frustrations, willingness (20 min)

> Goal: existing behavior, friction, money already spent. This section reveals the gap and the pocket.

9. Last time you wanted guidance from the Gita — or felt drawn to a deity — what did you actually do? Walk me through it step by step.
   - *Probe: Did you open an app? Call someone? Google? Sit quietly?*
   - *Probe: What were you hoping to feel afterwards?*

10. What apps do you currently have on your phone for anything devotional, spiritual, or wellness?
    - *(Let them list. Don't prompt names.)*
    - *Probe each one: when did you install it? When did you last open it? What's it good at? What's it bad at?*
    - *If Sri Mandir is mentioned: dig deep. What do you use it for? What's missing?*

11. YouTube, Instagram, WhatsApp — what role do those play in your devotional life?
    - *Probe: Who do you watch / follow? Bhavesh Bhimanathani? Dr. Acharya Prashant? A specific guru?*
    - *Probe: Do you forward devotional content on WhatsApp? To whom?*

12. What do you actually pay for in your spiritual life today?
    - *(Books, online classes, donations to temples, app subscriptions, e-puja services, retreats, malas, idols, gurudakshina, anything.)*
    - *Probe each: How much, how often, why that, why not something else?*

13. Tell me about the last time you felt genuinely anxious or stuck about something in your life. Did you turn to anything devotional for it? Why or why not?
    - *(This is the Ask Krishna question — in disguise. Don't reveal it.)*

14. **The forecast question:** Imagine a thing existed that — every morning — gave you a beautiful image of the deity of that day, one verse from the Gita with a short reflection, and let you ask any life question and get a Gita-grounded answer. Before I tell you anything more, what would you instinctively want from it? What would have to be true for you to use it every single day?

## Section 4 — Concept reaction (10 min)

> Goal: react to Smaran specifically. Show the 1-page concept brief (PDF or screen share). Read the value prop aloud.

**Concept brief to show:**
> *"Smaran (smah-RUN, स्मरण) — Sanskrit for 'remembrance.' Every morning: the deity of the day, one verse from the Gita with scholar commentary, and Ask Krishna — bring any life question and receive a Gita-grounded answer. Free tier: daily image + one-line verse. Premium ₹399/yr: full commentary, audio recited by [reciter's name], journal, festival packs, more questions. Devotee ₹999/yr: power-user limits + voice mode."*

15. First reaction, gut. What does this make you feel?
16. What pulls you in? What doesn't?
17. Anything in there that feels wrong, sectarian, or off-brand for the audience you imagine?
18. Who do you know who'd love this? Who'd hate this?
19. If you used it every day for 30 days — what would Day 15 look like? Are you still opening it? What changed?
20. **What's missing?** What would have to be added for you to be a paying user a year from now?

---

## Section 5 — Brand + pricing (10 min)

> Goal: validate the name, voice, price. Show three screens side by side.

### 21. Name test

Show four names on screen: **Smaran · Mandir · Darshan · Nitya**

- First reaction to each — one word?
- Which feels most premium? Most warm? Most "Indian without being touristy"?
- If you saw "Smaran" in the Play Store with no context, would you tap it?

### 22. Voice test

Show three notification copies (one Smaran, two distractors):

- A: *"Good morning. Today is Tuesday — Hanuman ji's day."*
- B: *"Hey! 🙏 Don't miss today's blessing — open now to start your day right!"*
- C: *"Tuesday wisdom: Hanuman's courage 🚩 Ready for divine inspiration?"*

- Which feels right for an app you'd use every day for years?
- Why?

### 23. Pricing reaction

Show the three tiers (Free / Premium ₹399/yr or ₹999 lifetime / Devotee ₹999/yr or ₹2,499 lifetime).

- First reaction — fair? Too high? Too low?
- Which would you pick for yourself, honestly?
- Annual or lifetime? Why?

### 24. Van Westendorp on Premium tier

> *"Forget the prices on screen for a moment. Imagine the Premium version — full commentary, audio, journal, festival packs, Ask Krishna up to 5 times a day."*

- At what yearly price would this be **so cheap** you'd doubt the quality?
- At what yearly price does it become **a bargain** — you'd buy without hesitating?
- At what yearly price does it start to feel **expensive**, but you'd still consider it?
- At what yearly price is it **too expensive** — you'd never buy?

*(Record four numbers in their currency. Diaspora respondents: ask in their local currency — USD/GBP/AUD.)*

### 25. Transactional layer test

> *"What if the app also let you do small things like — light a virtual diya for someone for ₹51, or sponsor a verse's audio recording for ₹101, or send a deity-of-day blessing to a friend's WhatsApp for ₹21. Does that feel right inside Smaran, or off-brand?"*

- *(This is the make-or-break question for the monetization path. Listen carefully. Note exact words.)*

## Section 6 — Wrap (5 min)

26. What did I not ask you that I should have?
27. Who in your life should I talk to next about this?
28. Is it OK if I follow up with one or two questions over WhatsApp later?

Thank. Send incentive within 24 hours.

## Segment-specific probe additions

Add 2-3 probes per segment, layered into the relevant sections.

### Practising Hindus in India

- In Section 2: *"Do you have an ishta-devata? Does it shift with the day of the week, or is it fixed?"*
- In Section 3 (Q11): *"WhatsApp forwarding — devotional images, good-morning messages — who sends, who receives?"*
- In Section 5 (Q25): probe whether e-puja / chadhava behavior is part of their life today.

### Indian diaspora (US/UK/AU)

- In Section 2: *"What do you miss most about home, devotionally?"*
- In Section 2: *"If you have kids — what do you want them to absorb that they're not getting in their everyday life here?"*
- In Section 3: *"Have you used apps designed for Indian audiences when you're abroad? How does the experience feel?"*

### Lapsed / seeker Hindus

- In Section 2: *"What did your devotional life look like 5-10 years ago? What changed?"*
- In Section 4: *"How preachy / sectarian / cultural does this concept feel to you on a scale of 1-10?"*
- In Section 5 (Q22): probe what kind of voice would make them feel **welcomed back** vs **lectured**.

### Sri Mandir users

- In Section 3 (Q10): *"Walk me through your last Sri Mandir session — what did you do, in order?"*
- In Section 3: *"What's frustrating about Sri Mandir? What do you wish existed?"*
- In Section 4: *"Is this different enough from Sri Mandir for you, or does it feel like the same thing?"*

## Post-interview ritual (do within 30 minutes)

1. Write the top 3 surprises in a notes app while fresh.
2. Capture 3 verbatim quotes that struck you.
3. Tag the participant: segment, willingness-to-pay band (low/mid/high), brand resonance (yes/maybe/no).
4. Add the row to the master interview log.

The synthesis is built from these field notes, not from listening back to 18 hours of audio.
