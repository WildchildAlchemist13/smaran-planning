# Smaran — Survey Instrument

> **Companion to**: `RESEARCH_PLAN.md` · **Length**: 5-8 min · **Target**: 150-250 launched, 100+ usable · **Tool**: Tally (preferred) or Google Forms.

---

## Survey design principles

- **5-8 minutes max** — anything longer and completion drops below 40%.
- **Mobile-first** — 70%+ of respondents will fill this on a phone.
- **Conditional logic** — Sri Mandir users see different follow-ups than seekers.
- **Open-text only at the end** — fatigue kills quality answers in the middle.
- **No "select your religion"** — Smaran isn't a religious census. Use practice descriptors instead.

### Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter (Premium tier)

> *"Forget the prices on screen. Imagine the Premium version — full commentary, audio, journal, festival packs, Ask Krishna up to 5 times a day."*

**F3.** At what yearly price would this be **so cheap** you'd doubt the quality? *(currency input)*

**F4.** At what yearly price does it become **a bargain** — you'd buy without hesitating? *(currency input)*

**F5.** At what yearly price does it start to feel **expensive**, but you'd still consider it? *(currency input)*

**F6.** At what yearly price is it **too expensive** — you'd never buy? *(currency input)*

*(Analysis: standard Van Westendorp — find the Optimal Price Point (intersection of "too cheap" and "too expensive") and the Range of Acceptable Prices.)*

## Analysis approach

**Quant cuts:**
- Segment × purchase intent (D1)
- Segment × Van Westendorp curves
- Segment × feature ranking top-3
- Practice frequency × purchase intent
- Geography × tier preference (tests the diaspora premium hypothesis)
- App ownership × purchase intent (does Sri Mandir use predict willingness?)

**Qual coding:**
- D2 ("yes" triggers) — affinity map → top 5 themes
- D3 ("worries") — affinity map → top 5 objections
- H1 ("what they do badly") — affinity map → product gap themes

**The four numbers we report to the board:**
1. **PI top-2 box** — % of respondents who said "definitely" or "probably" would download.
2. **Optimal Price Point** — Van Westendorp intersection.
3. **Top-ranked feature** — what V1 has to nail.
4. **Transactional layer YES rate** — % open to at least one ₹51-₹101 micro-payment.

If PI top-2 is ≥ 35%, OPP is ≥ ₹399, top feature isn't "wallpapers," and transactional YES is ≥ 40% — the $2-3k path is real. If any of those four fails, we adjust the model **before** the Week 3 build.

## Pilot before launch

Run the survey on 5 people from Cysters / family before going live. Time them. If anyone takes longer than 9 minutes — cut something. If anyone is confused — rewrite that question. Tally has built-in completion-rate analytics; check after the first 20 responses.
