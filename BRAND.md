# Smaran — Brand Guidelines

> The visual + verbal identity of Smaran. Use this brief when commissioning artists, designers, marketers, or writing any user-facing copy.

---

## 1. Brand Promise

> *"Smaran helps you remember the wisdom you already carry."*

Not a content app. Not a religious app. **A daily practice of remembrance.** Every interaction with the brand should feel like a calm, deliberate, sacred pause from the noise of modern life.

---

## 2. Brand Personality

| Smaran is | Smaran is not |
|---|---|
| Reverent | Preachy |
| Calm | Sleepy |
| Premium | Fancy |
| Rooted (Indian) | Sectarian (Hindu-only-feeling) |
| Wise | Old-fashioned |
| Modern | Trendy |
| Warm | Saccharine |
| Sacred | Solemn |
| Specific | Vague-spiritual |
| Personal | Corporate |

The closest aesthetic neighbors:
- **Calm** (restraint, mood, premium feel)
- **Hallow** (devotional warmth, ritual structure)
- **Stoic** (philosophical depth, journal format)
- **Aarogya / Headspace** (clean wellness UX)
- **Pichwai art** (Indian devotional with clean composition)

What we are **not** like:
- Sri Mandir (services-marketplace, busy UI)
- Astrology apps (gimmicky, fortune-telling vibe)
- Generic Hindu wallpaper apps (cluttered, ad-spammy)
- Western "yoga lifestyle" brands (cultural appropriation aesthetic)

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary

| Color | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Smaran Indigo** | `#1A1B3A` | Primary brand color — deep midnight blue. Krishna's color, the pre-dawn sky before morning ritual. Backgrounds, app icon base, navigation. |
| **Sacred Gold** | `#C9A961` | Accent — warm gold (not yellow). Sacred geometry, premium markers, premium tier badge, key CTAs. Used sparingly. |

### Secondary

| Color | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Ivory** | `#F5EFE3` | Surface / paper background for content (verses, commentary). Warmer than white, evokes old palm-leaf manuscripts. |
| **Sindoor Red** | `#A62024` | Devotional accent — sindoor / vermillion. Use only for special moments: festivals, streak milestones, mantras. Never for warnings or errors. |
| **Sage Green** | `#6B8E5C` | Tertiary accent — for "Devotee" tier, calm states, reflection prompts. Neem leaf / tulsi green. |
| **Charcoal** | `#2C2C2C` | Body text on Ivory backgrounds. Never use pure black. |
| **Soft Gray** | `#9A9A9A` | Secondary text, dividers, disabled states. |

### Color rules
- **Never use bright saffron** (#FF6B00 family) — too cliched, too "tourist Hinduism"
- **Never use neon or fluorescent** — wrong vibe entirely
- **Never use pure white** (#FFFFFF) — feels clinical; use Ivory
- **Never use pure black** — feels gothic; use Charcoal
- **Use gold sparingly** — if it's everywhere, it's nowhere

### Why this palette wins
- Indigo + gold = night sky + sacred fire = morning ritual aesthetic
- Ivory = manuscript / sacred text = reverent reading
- Avoids "Diwali colors" cliche while staying culturally grounded
- Premium app stores (Calm, Hallow, Stoic) all use deep dark + warm accent — this fits that pattern

## 4. Typography

### Display / Headlines (English)
- **Cormorant Garamond** OR **Marcellus** OR **Tenor Sans** — quality serif with Indian sensibility
- Use for: app name wordmark, verse numbers, section headers, hero copy

### Display / Devanagari
- **Tiro Devanagari Sanskrit** OR **Yatra One** OR **Mukta** — clean modern Devanagari with traditional grace
- Use for: स्मरण logo lockup, Sanskrit verses, Sanskrit subtitles

### Body Text (English)
- **Inter** OR **DM Sans** OR **Source Sans Pro** — clean modern sans
- Use for: commentary, journal, UI elements, all reading text
- Size: 16–18pt for body comfort; line height 1.6 for breathing room

### Body Text (Hindi/Devanagari)
- **Mukta** OR **Hind** OR **Tiro Devanagari Hindi** — modern Devanagari for body
- Use for: any Hindi UI text, transliterations, subtitles

### Type rules
- Display always uses serif (English) or quality Devanagari (Sanskrit/Hindi)
- Body always uses sans
- Generous line height, generous letter spacing on display
- Italic only for Sanskrit transliteration (e.g., *karmaṇy-evādhikāras te*)

## 5. Logo Direction

### Primary lockup
```
[स्मरण]
SMARAN
```

- Devanagari **स्मरण** in chosen Devanagari font, slightly larger
- "SMARAN" in chosen serif, smaller, all caps, tracked wide
- Centered alignment

### Optional symbol mark (for app icon, social avatar)
Recommended directions to brief the designer:

1. **Bindu** — a single dot inside a thin gold circle. Bindu is the source of all in Hindu cosmology — the seed of remembrance.
2. **Diya flame** — minimal stylized flame in gold on indigo. Smriti = spark of memory.
3. **Lotus opening** — geometric, minimal lotus petal arrangement — opening to wisdom.
4. **Sanskrit aksharas** — a stylized "स" or just a single character treated as a logo mark.

**Recommendation**: start with **Bindu** — most minimal, most ownable, hardest to make ugly, scales perfectly to small sizes (Play Store icon, notification icon).

### Logo don'ts
- No deity faces in the logo (sacrilegious to use as brand mark)
- No om symbol (overused, cliched)
- No lotus + om + flame combos (visually busy, Diwali-card aesthetic)
- No script fonts that look like "Indian restaurant menu" lettering
- No hand-drawn / wobbly fonts

## 6. Visual Style (for artists + designers)

### Composition principles
- **Generous white space** — never fill the canvas
- **Centered or rule-of-thirds** for full-screen deity art
- **Visual breathing room** at top + bottom of deity illustrations (UI overlays will sit there)
- **Single subject focus** — one deity, not crowded scenes
- **Symmetry where appropriate** — deities traditionally rendered with symmetry

### Style direction for deity art
**Reference styles** (good to send to artists):
- Pichwai painting tradition (especially modern Pichwai by artists like Pooja Singhal, Avadhesh Sharma)
- Modern minimalist Hindu illustration (search "modern devotional art" on Behance)
- Tanjore-inspired with restrained gold accents
- Classical iconography executed with contemporary craft

**Avoid**:
- Marvel-comic-style heroic posing
- Anime / chibi cuteness
- Photorealistic 3D renders (uncanny valley)
- Anything that looks AI-generated (we will reject these on sight)
- "Spiritual stock photo" generic mandalas

### Lighting + mood
- **Warm light** (golden hour, oil lamp, dawn)
- **Never cold blue light**
- Atmosphere should evoke pre-dawn or dusk — the liminal hours of ritual

## 7. Voice + Tone

### How Smaran speaks (in app, marketing, notifications)

**Voice attributes**:
- Reverent
- Calm
- Specific (never generic-spiritual)
- Warm (not cold or academic)
- Concise (never flowery)

### Examples

**Notification copy**

✅ *"Good morning. Today is Tuesday — Hanuman ji's day."*
❌ *"Wake up and start your spiritual journey today!"*

✅ *"5 reflections shared today. Krishna teaches restraint as much as inquiry. Return tomorrow with what's still alive in you."*
❌ *"You've used up all your free messages! Upgrade to Premium for unlimited!"*

✅ *"30 days. The practice is becoming yours."*
❌ *"Awesome streak! Keep it up champion!"*

**Marketing copy**

✅ *"5,000-year-old practice. 30 seconds a day. Smaran."*
❌ *"Discover the secrets of ancient Indian wisdom!"*

✅ *"We forget. Smaran helps us remember."*
❌ *"Transform your life with the power of the Bhagavad Gita!"*

**App store description opening**

✅ *"Smaran (smah-RUN, स्मरण) — Sanskrit for 'remembrance.' The 5,000-year-old practice Krishna taught in the Bhagavad Gita: remember the divine in every moment. Now, in your pocket."*
❌ *"India's #1 Bhagavad Gita app! Get daily quotes, mantras, horoscopes, and more!"*

### Voice rules
- **Always italicize Sanskrit transliteration**: *karmaṇy-evādhikāras te*
- **Always provide translation alongside** untranslated Sanskrit terms first time used
- **Never use exclamation points** in primary copy (max 1 per page in marketing, never in app)
- **Never use emojis in primary brand copy** (occasional 🪔 or ॐ in notifications acceptable; never 🙏 — too memey)
- **Use 2nd person ("you")** in app and marketing
- **Refer to deities respectfully** ("Krishna," "Hanuman ji," "Lord Shiva" — not just first names always)
- **Never use Sanskrit just to sound exotic** — every Sanskrit word should add meaning

## 9. Things to Never Do

| Never | Why |
|---|---|
| AI-generated faces of deities | Cultural sacrilege; users will detect; brand-killing |
| Use the OM symbol as logo | Overused, cliched, dilutes brand |
| Bright saffron / Diwali-poster colors | Tourist-Hinduism aesthetic |
| Crowded, busy layouts | Wrong for ritual product |
| Pop-up paywalls in middle of ritual flow | Breaks the sacred moment |
| Aggressive notifications ("Don't break your streak!") | Anti-mindful, wrong for Smaran |
| Generic stock photos of Indian temples | Inauthentic, overused |
| Lotus + flame + diya emoji combos in brand copy | Overdesigned |
| Comparisons to other religions | Stay in your lane |
| Christianity-flavored language ("blessing," "prayer," "faith") | Use Sanskrit-rooted terms — darshan, sankalp, dhyan, smaran |
| English exoticization ("the divine feminine," "manifestation") | Avoid Western pop-spirituality vocabulary |
